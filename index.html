Being part of a family is being part of a team - there is give and take in everything, just like life. These guidelines help us all live happily and respectfully together, creating a peaceful and enjoyable home environment for everyone.

  • Dinner time connection. We don't mind when you go out for dinner, but we love having dinner with you when you are home. If you don't like what we cook, please help us with suggestions.
  • Contributing to our home. We all pitch in to keep our home tidy and comfortable. Taking initiative with tasks like clearing your dishes or tidying your space really helps everyone, and it's wonderful when we notice things that need doing together.
  • Keeping our spaces tidy. We take responsibility for clearing up after ourselves, like putting blankets away and tossing snack wrappers in the trash, to keep our shared spaces pleasant for everyone.
  • Respectful communication. We always aim to treat each other with kindness and respect in our home. This means communicating calmly, even when we feel frustrated, and finding ways to work through disagreements together. Please also let us know important information like if you are not coming for dinner or you will be late.
  • Respect for belongings. We respect each other's belongings and privacy by always asking before using something that isn't ours, and putting them back when you are finished.
  • Sharing household resources. When it comes to laundry, we help keep things flowing by doing our washing in a timely manner and making sure the washer and dryer are available for others when we're done.
  • Home time. School nights home by 10.30pm and Friday and Saturday by midnight. If you don't respect these times or communicate with us, groundings may occur.

We know it's difficult for you to get a steady job with all your activities, so we are giving you an allowance.

  • Allowance Amount: **$25** per week.
  • Payment Day: Every **Friday** automatically into your bank account.

  • Budgeting is Key: We won't give you extra money if you ask.
  • This is a privilege. Your allowance can be stopped if you do not consistently follow the agreements we've made for schoolwork and house rules.
